About

I am a software developer, artist, and educator. I went to school to be a math teacher, and in the classroom I developed an interest in data-driven pedagogy. I leveraged that experience to work as a data analyst, where I spent time developing internal tools. This launched my career as a software developer.

I'm particularly interested in creating tech for social good, cybersecurity, and fiber arts.

Currently, I'm working at The Center for Effective Philanthropy building data pipelines for survey data.

Philosophy

I believe that technology should solve real problems for real people. My teaching background taught me that good tools should be clear and accessible to everyone, not just experts. I strive to build software that works reliably, respects users' needs, and contributes something meaningful to the world.
